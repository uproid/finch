# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders of the Finch framework community pledge to make participation in our project and community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

* **Demonstrating empathy and kindness** toward other people
* **Being respectful** of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
* **Giving and gracefully accepting** constructive feedback
* **Accepting responsibility** and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from the experience
* **Focusing on what is best** not just for us as individuals, but for the overall community
* **Using welcoming and inclusive language** in all communications
* **Being patient** with newcomers and those learning the framework
* **Providing helpful and constructive code reviews** that focus on improvement
* **Celebrating successes** and acknowledging contributions of all sizes
* **Sharing knowledge freely** and helping others learn Dart and the Finch framework

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment of any form
* Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without their explicit permission
* Deliberately intimidating, stalking, or following someone
* Violent threats or language directed against another person
* Incitement of violence or harassment towards any individual, including encouraging a person to commit self-harm
* Unwelcome comments regarding a person's lifestyle choices and practices
* Sustained disruption of community events, including talks, presentations, and online discussions
* Pattern of inappropriate social contact, such as requesting/assuming inappropriate levels of intimacy with others
* Continued one-on-one communication after requests to cease
* "Doxxing" or sharing personal information without consent
* Spam or excessive self-promotion unrelated to the Finch framework
* Submitting intentionally malicious or harmful code
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Scope of Application

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including but not limited to:

* **GitHub repositories**: Issues, pull requests, discussions, code reviews, and commits
* **Communication channels**: Discord, Slack, mailing lists, forums, or any official community platforms
* **Social media**: When representing the Finch project on Twitter, LinkedIn, Reddit, or other platforms
* **Events**: Conferences, meetups, workshops, hackathons (both virtual and in-person)
* **Direct communication**: Emails, direct messages, or any communication between community members regarding the project

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include using an official email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders and maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it by contacting the project maintainers at:

* **Email**: [Insert contact email]
* **GitHub**: Submit a confidential report through GitHub's reporting features
* **Alternative contact**: [Insert alternative contact if available]

All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality.

### What to Include in a Report

When reporting an incident, please include as much of the following information as possible:

1. **Your contact information** (so we can follow up with you)
2. **Names (real, nicknames, or pseudonyms)** of any individuals involved
3. **Description of the incident**, including:
   - When and where it occurred
   - Whether the incident is ongoing
   - Context surrounding the incident
4. **Any available records** (screenshots, links, messages, etc.)
5. **Any other information** you believe would be helpful
6. **Whether you would like to remain anonymous** to the person being reported

### After Reporting

After receiving a report, the project maintainers will:

1. **Acknowledge receipt** of your report within 48 hours
2. **Review the incident** thoroughly and impartially
3. **Contact all parties involved** (if appropriate) to gather more information
4. **Determine appropriate action** based on the circumstances
5. **Follow up with the reporter** about the resolution (respecting privacy constraints)
6. **Take corrective action** as deemed necessary

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time (e.g., 30 days). This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time (e.g., 90 days). No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## Appeals Process

If you believe you have been unfairly accused of violating this Code of Conduct, you have the right to appeal:

1. **Submit your appeal** in writing to the project maintainers within 14 days of receiving notice of the enforcement action
2. **Include your reasoning** for why you believe the decision should be reconsidered
3. **Provide any additional evidence** or context that may not have been considered
4. The appeals will be reviewed by community leaders who were not involved in the original decision
5. **Final decision** will be communicated within 30 days of receiving the appeal

## Community Guidelines for Contributors

### For All Contributors

* **Be patient and welcoming** to newcomers
* **Assume good faith** in others' contributions and communications
* **Ask questions** before making assumptions
* **Provide context** when reporting issues or requesting features
* **Write clear commit messages** that explain the "why" behind changes
* **Test your code** before submitting pull requests
* **Follow the project's coding standards** and style guidelines
* **Be open to feedback** on your contributions

### For Code Reviewers

* **Focus on the code, not the person** who wrote it
* **Provide specific, actionable feedback** rather than vague criticism
* **Explain the "why"** behind your suggestions
* **Acknowledge good work** and improvements
* **Be timely** in your reviews when possible
* **Suggest alternatives** rather than just pointing out problems
* **Be respectful** of different skill levels and approaches

### For Maintainers

* **Lead by example** in all interactions
* **Respond to issues and PRs** in a timely manner
* **Provide clear guidance** for contributions
* **Be transparent** about project decisions and direction
* **Recognize and celebrate** community contributions
* **Enforce the Code of Conduct** fairly and consistently
* **Communicate clearly** about breaking changes and deprecations

## Technical Conduct

### Code Quality

* Write clean, maintainable, and well-documented code
* Follow Dart best practices and the Finch framework conventions
* Include appropriate tests for new features and bug fixes
* Ensure backward compatibility when possible
* Document breaking changes clearly

### Security

* **Never include** sensitive information (passwords, API keys, tokens) in code or commits
* **Report security vulnerabilities** privately to maintainers before public disclosure
* **Follow security best practices** when contributing code
* **Be responsible** when discussing potential security issues

### Intellectual Property

* **Only submit code** that you have the right to contribute
* **Respect licenses** of dependencies and third-party code
* **Give credit** where credit is due
* **Don't plagiarize** code from other sources without proper attribution

## Diversity and Inclusion

The Finch project values diversity and is committed to creating an inclusive environment. We welcome contributions from people of all backgrounds and identities. We strive to:

* Use inclusive language in documentation and code
* Make our community accessible to people with disabilities
* Support contributors from different time zones and regions
* Provide documentation in multiple languages when possible
* Create opportunities for contributors at all skill levels

## Questions and Clarifications

If you have questions about this Code of Conduct or need clarification on any points:

* **Open a GitHub Discussion** in the community forum
* **Contact the maintainers** directly via email
* **Review the FAQ** (if available) in the documentation
